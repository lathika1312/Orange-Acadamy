What is Medical Coding?

Medical Coding involves extracting patient information from medical records such as doctor’s notes, lab results etc. and transforming them into universally accepted alphanumeric codes.

Why is Medical Coding needed?

Codification of a patient’s medical information plays a crucial role in assisting doctors with diagnosis and treatment. It helps insurers determine the accuracy of claims and provide appropriate reimbursements. Finally, governments can also access this health data to formulate health policies.

What is the role of a Medical Coder?

The role of a Medical Coder is to assign codes to diagnoses and procedures in inpatient and outpatient records. They also review patient records and assign codes for reimbursement and data analysis.
